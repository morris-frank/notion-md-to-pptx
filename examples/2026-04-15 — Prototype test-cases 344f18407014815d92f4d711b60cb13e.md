# Prototype tests

## Lifecycle

#### One-off diagnostic engagement

A customer hires Soilytix for a single concrete problem on a specific field or field set, with one sampling moment and one delivered result.

**What exists**

- Customer
- Commercial engagement
- Approved sampling setup
- One campaign
- One analysis package
- One delivered output

**What this validates**

- The model can represent the simplest sellable customer lifecycle.
- A commercial container can exist without repeated sessions.
- A single operational episode can still produce multiple downstream artifacts.

**Real validator**

- “A customer sends one field campaign and expects one report back.”

---

## Investigation

#### Designed trial with treatment and control

A customer runs a field trial where treatment groups matter, and the scientific framing is about comparison rather than standalone description.

**What exists**

- Customer engagement
- Trial / study definition
- Treatment structure
- Campaign at one time point
- Field-to-treatment assignment
- Comparative EvidenceSet

**What this validates**

- Investigation framing may need durable identity.
- Campaign is subordinate to a designed comparison.
- Grouping variables and trial structure are first-class, not incidental metadata.

**Real validator**

- “Can we represent a real A/B field trial without collapsing it into just a bag of samples?”

---

## Lifecycle

#### Longitudinal monitoring program

A customer wants the same areas monitored across multiple campaigns over time, so continuity matters more than any single campaign.

**What exists**

- Customer relationship over time
- Stable field set or area set
- Repeated campaigns
- Timestamped observations
- Cross-campaign comparability
- Time-series reporting

**What this validates**

- The model supports repeat delivery against stable spatial scope.
- Campaign ordering and temporal identity matter.
- One investigation frame may span multiple service sessions.

**Real validator**

- “Track this orchard’s soil biology every season and show change over time.”

## Product

#### Biodiversity monitoring & reporting product

A customer buys a reportable biodiversity product, where the main thing purchased is a reporting artifact rather than `MeasurementSet`.

**What exists**

- Customer-facing product definition
- Sampling design
- Sequencing workflow
- Optional geo-data workflow
- Biodiversity report artifact
- Regulatory or standards context

**What this validates**

- The model distinguishes product from workflow.
- Report artifacts can be primary deliverables.
- Regulatory context may shape setup and output without changing the lab core.

**Real validator**

- “Customer needs biodiversity indicators reported in a format aligned with an external framework.”

---

## Product

#### Soil health recommendation product

A customer does not just want measurements — they want an interpreted recommendation that combines multiple evidence streams into advice.

**What exists**

- Customer request for an outcome
- Sequencing-based evidence
- Physicochemical evidence
- Shared soil health score
- Recommendation

**What this validates**

- Multiple analytical paths can converge into one interpretation object.
- Scores and recommendations are different domain outputs.
- Customer value may sit in advisory synthesis, not in the assays themselves.

**Real validator**

- “Customer wants yield improvement recommendations, not just a microbiome readout.”

---

## Investigation

#### Pathogen suppression setup

A customer wants a richer investigation with optional modules, classification layers, predictive models, and a final suppressiveness interpretation.

**What exists**

- Multi-amplicon setup
- Optional qPCR
- Optional geo-data and weather enrichment
- Taxonomic and functional classification
- Statistical analysis layer
- Pathogen suppression scoring artifact

**What this validates**

- Some offerings are composed analytical programs, not single tests.
- Optional modules can materially change the meaning of the output.
- Final deliverables can be scored interpretations with explainability layers.

**Real validator**

- “Can the model describe a real suppressiveness workflow without hiding critical optional branches?”

---

## Product

#### Trial fairness / homogeneity certificate

A customer wants proof that comparison areas were comparable before interpreting trial outcomes.

**What exists**

- Trial-oriented customer setup
- Biology evidence
- Chemistry evidence
- Optional geo-data evidence
- Fairness / homogeneity certificate

**What this validates**

- Not every product is a diagnosis or recommendation.
- Some outputs are precondition artifacts for later decision-making.
- The same operational inputs can support a very different customer product.

**Real validator**

- “Customer needs to defend that treatment and control were comparable before claiming trial effects.”

---

## Exception

#### Sequence-as-a-service characterisation request

A customer sends substrate or product material and asks, “What is in this?”, without a real trial design or longitudinal frame.

**What exists**

- Commercial request
- Material intake
- Sequencing and processing
- Characterisation output
- Little or no true study design

**What this validates**

- The model must handle thin or absent investigation framing.
- Study-like objects may need to be optional or lightweight.
- Some jobs are characterisation requests, not scientific programs.

**Real validator**

- “Substrate QC” or “product characterisation” should not require a fake trial structure to exist.
